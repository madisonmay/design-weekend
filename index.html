Design Phases

From casual conversations to final presentations.

Empathize

Gain a deeper understanding of the people of the people you're designing for. Learn what needs and values they hold, and identify areas of opportunity for improving their lives.

Conceptualize

Using what you learned from your interactions with students and teachers, build on the areas of opportunities to generate a wide range of possible solutions. Then narrow down the pool to a few promising concepts.

Develop

Select a final idea to pursue and flush out the details of your concept, then prepare to communicate your idea to the world.

FAQ

What's the cost?

The event is completely free to attend. We'll even feed you and help out with transportation!

Why design for education?

Olin College was founded 10 years ago with the goal of revolutionizing engineering education, and we'd like to spread that spirit of educational experimentation.

How can I get to Olin?

If you can't drive to 1000 Olin Way, Needham, MA, we will be running a shuttle to and from the Eliot T Station (Green D Line).

Do I need a team?

Nope! We'll be sending out a preregistration survey to set up teams of people with diverse backgrounds and abilities.

Who can participate?

The event is for current undergraduate students, but we're also currently looking for professional designers, K-12 students, and teachers to help make the event possible.

How large will the event be?

We're planning for around 100 undergraduate students.
